Tips, Notes and Special remark
  • It's better if you can carry a stock of water (1L per person) and food products.
  • Start as early as possible
  • Do not carry Plastic items (Minimize), Make sure you bring back everything you took.
  • Be aware of wild elephants.
  • Might need use of leech repellents in rainy days.
  • Ask directions from locals

Trail details

Yahangala is the most difficult hike we have ever went on. It is a plain rock with few amount of trees. It is believed that the great king Rawana is sleeping inside this rock, with "Yahan" meaning bed in Sinhala.

We gathered in kandy around 10 am and took a Mahiyanganaya bus.

We got down from Ududumbara at around 12.30 pm.

There is a bus service from Ududumbara to Kalugala once every hour. We had missed it by about 5 minutes. Therefore we took a 3 wheeler and he charged us around 800 rupees. The villagers gave us instructions regarding the path.

We started the hike around 1 pm.

The last house we met was of Piyasena Maama's. It is better to obtain his phone number in case of an emergency.

The initial part of the hike is a hill which is quite steep and tiring. It takes about 1 1/2 hours climb this part. You can fill up your water bottles from a water line in the hill. This water is taken from a natural water resource.

At the top of this hill there is a small "Dewalaya"/shrine made out of bricks.At this point you must turn right and walk upto a giant tree.If you turn right from this point you will walk to plain from which Yahangala is clearly visible for the first time during the hike. If you turn left you will find the path to Yahangala.

** IMPORTANT : It is crucial to turn right at the Dewalaya. This was not mentioned in any of the guides we referred prior to the hike. We took the straight path from Dewalaya and it took us about 1 hour to come back to the Dewalaya.

It was about 4.15 when we started the climb of Yahangala. The climb is quite hard because it is very steep.Also since we were camping on top of Yahangala, we had to carry the tent and firewood since no wood can be found on top of Yahangala. This made the journey quite hard.

It is important that you climb this as a team. There are some places you can not pass through wearing a bag. It is crucial that you have had some experience in hiking before this.

Please do not attempt this as your first hike. The path is slippery with sand and gravel. Some of us wore slippers while some were bare footed.There are two places that are specially hard. Both these places are on the side of the rock and fall is directly below. At the first place there are trees to hold for support. At the second place there is only a shaking but stable rock for support (please watch the video).

It was about 6.15pm when we reached the top. We prepared the tent and the camp fire. There are plain rocks to light the camp fire. After the sunset it starts getting windy. We had our dinner inside the tent. Please remember to extinguish the fire since there is a danger of wildfire.

Make sure the tent is quite strong because the wind was really strong after midnight. The night sky and the Mahiyangana town in lights made for a stupendous view. In the morning we watched the sunrise and had our breakfast. We started to climb down after the winds had quitened a bit.

It only took us about an hour to reach Dewalaya. We walked to the Kalugala village and took the bus to Ududumbara and another bus to Kandy.
